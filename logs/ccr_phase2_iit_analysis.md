## ABSTRACT

This paper presents Integrated Information Theory (IIT) 4.0. IIT aims to account for the properties of experience in physical (operational) terms. It identifies the essential properties of experience (axioms), infers the necessary and sufficient properties that its substrate must satisfy (postulates), and expresses them in mathematical terms. In principle, the postulates can be applied to any system of units in a state to determine whether it is conscious, to what degree, and in what way. IIT offers a parsimonious explanation for empirical findings about neural correlates of consciousness and allows for inferences about consciousness in non-standard observers, including infants, non-human animals, brain organoids, and machines. IIT is falsifiable on empirical and theoretical grounds: evidence about neural substrates can rule out the theory, as can the existence of substrates entailing 'unfolding' of experiences contradicting the axioms.
